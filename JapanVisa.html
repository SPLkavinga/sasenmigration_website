Information about Japan Visa

Documentation & Qualifications for a Japan work Visa

To qualify for a Japan Work Visa, applicants must meet specific requirements depending on their visa type. Below you'll find the general qualifications, steps, and documents needed.

Special Work Visa for Graduates

This visa is for those who hold a degree in IT and JLPT/NAT Level -5 certificate and degree holders with International Affairs and /or English content in the degree programme along with JLPT/ NAT Level -5 certificate are eligible to apply for this visa.

SSW (Specified Skilled Workers)

This visa best suits those who do not have a particular skill and work experience. The jobs available for this visa category include Care giving, Agriculture and Food industry. The applicant is expected to have JLPT, JFT (Basic A2), NAT or JLCT N4 qualification at the point of applying for the visa and get through skill proficiency test related to the job of his/ her choice. The age limit for this visa is 35 years. SSW, Skill Proficiency Test is conducted in Sri Lanka for Agriculture, Food Service and Care Giving. The applicant will be given the necessary support to face the skill proficiency test.

Training Visa

The age range for this visa is 19-35 years. Work experience is not required to apply. Japanese language proficiency is required therefore, it is a must that applicant have JLPT/JFT/ NAT OR JLCT qualification at the point of applying.

  • You must have a confirmed job offer from a Japanese company or institution.
  • The employer will act as your sponsor for the visa.

Degree or Certificate: A university degree or specialized diploma related to the job is usually required.

Experience: For skilled labor roles, proof of relevant work experience (3–10 years, depending on the field) may suffice.

  • Your employer must apply for a COE from the Japanese Immigration Bureau.
  • The COE confirms that you meet the immigration requirements for the intended job.

Qualifications vary by visa type. Common categories include:

  • Specialist in Humanities/International Services: Requires a degree/experience in fields like education, marketing, translation, or international business.
  • Engineer/IT Specialist: Requires a relevant technical degree; certifications (CISCO, Oracle) add value.
  • Skilled Labor Visa: Requires formal training or experience in specialized fields (cooking, construction, manufacturing).
  • Specified Skilled Worker (SSW): For industries with labor shortages (nursing care, construction, hospitality). May require passing a skill and language test.
  • Instructor Visa: Requires qualifications to teach foreign languages or specialized subjects.

Proficiency in Japanese (JLPT N4 or above) is preferred, especially for roles involving local interaction.

Some roles (e.g., SSW) mandate specific Japanese language capabilities.

  • A valid passport with sufficient validity.
  • Academic and professional certificates.
  • Contract or offer letter from the employer.
  • Other documents required for the COE application.
